Quiche

Description

Making quiche may feel intimidating, but it needn't be if you get your liquid ratios correct. In contrast to other quiches you may have had - light and souffle-like in texture - this recipe produces a reasonably dense body when fully cooked.

As for ingredients, those fall into four categories: cheese, vegetables, herbs & spices, and if you desire, some type(s) of meat. For illustration purposes, the recipe below is vegetarian, and assumes you're using a frozen pie shell.

Ingredients

  • Pie shell
  • 7 eggs
  • 7/8 c light cream
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • black pepper to taste
  • 2 T olive oil
  • 1 white or yellow onion, chopped
  • 8-12 small mushrooms, sliced
  • 6 oz sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 8 oz cheddar, grated
  • 1 T butter

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, mix the eggs, light cream, salt and as much pepper as you like.
  3. In a medium skillet, saute the onions until they start to brown. Add the mushrooms and continue sauteeing until they have expressed most of their liquid. Add sun-dried tomatoes, stir and remove from heat.
  4. Remove frozen pie shell from freezer and place on a baking sheet.
  5. Layer 1/3rd of cheddar, half of the sauteed vegetables and half of your egg-cream mixture. Repeat again, topping with your final 1/3rd of grated cheddar.
  6. Dot the surface of the quiche with small amounts of butter (these will spread during baking and help to prevent a glossy finished surface).
  7. Place the quiche on the middle rack in the oven.
  8. Bake for 35-50 min; it's ready when the middle of the quiche is set and the quiche itself is starting to rise.
  9. Cool on a rack. Serves 4-8. Eat immediately or refrigerate - it's good either way!
